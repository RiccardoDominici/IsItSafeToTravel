  <action>
Extend `src/pipeline/calibration/calibrate.ts` by adding a `--bias` CLI flag (check `process.argv.includes('--bias')`). When invoked with `--bias`, the script:

1. Loads `data/calibration/deviation-report.json`

2. Groups countries by three dimensions:
   - **By UN Region** (Africa, Americas, Asia, Europe, Oceania): compute per-group mean signed deviation, mean absolute deviation, count, and std dev
   - **By Risk Tier** (safe: ref 7-10, moderate: ref 4-7, dangerous: ref 1-4): same stats
   - **By Geopolitical Bloc** (NATO, EU, BRICS, G7, Non-aligned): same stats

3. Identifies bias patterns using threshold: a group has systematic bias if `|mean_signed_delta| > 0.5` AND the group has at least 5 countries

4. For each biased group, lists the top 3 countries contributing most to the bias (highest |delta| in the group)

5. Writes `data/calibration/bias-analysis.md` with the following structure:

```markdown
# Bias Analysis Report

Generated: {ISO 8601}
Based on: deviation-report.json ({N} countries)

## Summary Statistics

- Global MAD: {value}
- Global Mean Signed Deviation: {value}
- Pearson r: {value}

## Regional Bias

| Region | Countries | Mean Signed Deviation | MAD | Bias? |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|-----|-------|
| Africa | {n} | {val} | {val} | {Yes/No} |
...

### Biased Regions Detail
(For each biased region, list top 3 outlier countries with delta values)

## Risk Tier Bias

| Tier | Countries | Mean Signed Deviation | MAD | Bias? |
|------|-----------|----------------------|-----|-------|
...

## Geopolitical Bloc Bias

| Bloc | Countries | Mean Signed Deviation | MAD | Bias? |
|------|-----------|----------------------|-----|-------|
...

## Key Findings

(Auto-generated bullet points summarizing which groups show bias, direction, and magnitude)

## Limitations

- Reference dataset partially overlaps with scoring engine sources (GPI, INFORM, WB)
- Pearson r interpretation: r > 0.95 suggests tautological overlap; r < 0.70 suggests scoring issues
- Small territories excluded (no reference data available)
```

Extend `src/pipeline/calibration/calibrate.ts` by adding a `--proposals` CLI flag. When invoked with `--proposals`, the script:

1. Loads `data/calibration/deviation-report.json`, `data/calibration/bias-analysis.md` (parse the Key Findings), and `src/pipeline/config/weights.json`

2. Generates exactly 2 weight adjustment proposals:

**Proposal A: Pillar Rebalancing**
- Read current pillar weights from weights.json: conflict 0.30, crime 0.25, health 0.20, governance 0.15, environment 0.10
- Based on bias analysis findings, propose adjusted pillar weights that address the largest identified bias
- Example adjustments to consider: if crime pillar is underweighted (South Africa over-rated), increase crime weight; if conflict pillar is causing over-penalization of politically unstable but otherwise safe countries, reduce conflict weight
- Keep weights summing to 1.0
- For projection: do NOT re-run the full scoring engine. Instead, estimate impact using a simplified model: for each country in deviation-report.json, estimate the score change direction and approximate magnitude based on which pillar changed
- Show: proposed weights, rationale tied to specific bias finding, projected MAD change (direction + estimated magnitude), top 5 most affected countries

**Proposal B: Advisory Sub-Weight Rebalancing**
- Read current advisory sub-weights from weights.json conflict.indicators (advisory_level_us, advisory_level_uk, etc.)
- If bias analysis shows Western-centric advisory bias (e.g., NATO/EU countries systematically scored differently), propose redistribution
- Consider: equalizing advisory weights across all 37 sources, or grouping by geographic diversity (Western, Asian, Latin American, African sources) and weighting groups equally
- Show: proposed sub-weights, rationale, projected impact, top 5 most affected countries
